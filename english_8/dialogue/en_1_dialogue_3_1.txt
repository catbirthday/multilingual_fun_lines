Character 2: Mmm, that's... that's actually a beautiful question. I mean, they probably do—they have neurons in their arms that can make independent decisions, like, each arm kind of has its own mini-brain, which must create this distributed consciousness we can't even imagine.

Character 2: Ah, well, that's the tragic part—most only live like one to two years, maybe five for the giant Pacific octopus, which seems so unfair for something that brilliant, you know?

Character 2: Hmm, for a day? Absolutely. I'd love to experience that distributed consciousness, to taste with my skin, to squeeze through impossibly tiny spaces—but honestly, knowing it would only be for such a brief lifetime would probably break my heart.
