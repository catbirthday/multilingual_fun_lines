=== Translated Dialogue ===

Character 1 [en]: So wait, are they actually listening to everything we say through our phones?

Character 2 [en]: Oh man, um, that's... that's actually a super complicated question. I mean, technically your phone isn't, like, constantly streaming audio to some government server or anything, but there are definitely ways that, uh, intelligence agencies can access devices if they really want to, you know?

Character 1 [en]: [very meekly] I knew it. I should throw mine away.

Character 2 [en]: Whoa whoa, hold on, that's— that's probably not necessary. Look, most people aren't actually interesting enough for anyone to bother with, frankly. The resources it would take to monitor everyone's conversations would be, mmm, absolutely astronomical.

Character 1 [en]: [curious] But what about that Snowden guy? Didn't he prove they do it?

Character 2 [en]: Yeah, so, okay— Snowden revealed mass data collection programs, which is different from, uh, actively listening to conversations. They were more like, hmm, scooping up metadata— who you called, when, for how long— rather than actually recording what you said, if that makes sense?

Character 1 [en]: Actually, never mind. Can they see through my laptop camera?

Character 2 [en]: Ugh, you're jumping around here but, er, yes, technically malware can hijack webcams, which is why, you know, some people put tape over them. But again, you'd have to be specifically targeted, and honestly most hackers are after credit card numbers, not, like, watching random people eat cereal.

Character 1 [en]: My ex definitely hacked my Instagram though.

Character 2 [en]: That's... wait, that's completely different. Um, social media hacking is usually just someone guessing your password or, I dunno, maybe you left yourself logged in somewhere? That's not really intelligence gathering, that's more like, erm, personal drama territory.

Character 1 [en]: Do you think aliens are involved in any of this?

Character 2 [en]: Okay so now we're— we're really going off the rails here. Haha, I mean, intelligence gathering is definitely a real thing that governments do, but, uh, bringing aliens into it kind of undermines the actual legitimate concerns about privacy and surveillance that we should probably be focusing on.

Character 1 [en]: Sorry, I just remembered something. What's metadata again?

Character 2 [en]: Oh, alright, back to metadata— think of it like the envelope of a letter rather than what's inside. So they can see who you're talking to, when, where you were, how long the call lasted, but not necessarily the actual content of what you discussed, you know? Still invasive but, mmm, different.

Character 1 [en]: That's still creepy. Should I use a VPN?

Character 2 [en]: Actually, yeah, a VPN isn't a bad idea for general privacy, though it's not, like, some magic invisibility cloak or anything. It basically just routes your internet traffic through another server so your ISP can't see what sites you're visiting, but, erm, the VPN company itself could theoretically see everything, so you gotta trust them.

Character 1 [en]: Wait, can they hear us right now? Through Alexa?

Character 2 [en]: [cry] Oh god, we're back to this— look, Alexa only starts recording after it hears its wake word, though there have been, ugh, some incidents where it misheard things and recorded by accident, which is genuinely concerning and why I personally don't have one in my bedroom.

Character 1 [en]: [playful] Alexa, are you spying on me?

Character 2 [en]: Haha, okay, you're messing around now, but seriously though, the real intelligence gathering happens through, um, much more boring methods— credit card purchases, location data from your phone, social media posts you voluntarily share. The stuff we give away freely is actually way more valuable than anything they'd get from, like, secretly recording us.
