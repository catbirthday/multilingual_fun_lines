=== Original (English) ===

Character 1: You ever use one of those fancy espresso machines? The ones that cost like three grand?

Character 2: Those things are pure marketing nonsense. You can get the exact same quality from a two-hundred dollar machine if you know what you're doing. The expensive ones just have more chrome and buttons to make rich people feel special.

Character 1: But the pressure gauges and temperature controls must make a difference.

Character 2: [celoso] Sure, if you believe the hype from people who already dropped their money on them. They'll swear it changed their life because admitting they wasted three thousand dollars would hurt too much. Classic sunk cost fallacy right there.

Character 1: [hablando sonriente] My neighbor swears by his Breville Oracle Touch.

Character 2: Your neighbor probably can't tell the difference between Ethiopian single origin and gas station swill. These machines prey on insecurity - making people think they need professional equipment to make decent coffee when really they just need to learn basic technique.

Character 1: [exhausto] God, is there anything you actually believe in?

Character 2: [risa histérica] Belief? That's rich! No, I believe in observable facts and repeatable results. Everything else is just people trying to sell you something or make themselves feel better about their choices.

Character 1: [lentamente] What about that vintage camera equipment you collect?

Character 2: That's different - old lenses have actual optical qualities you can't replicate digitally. But even then, half the collectors are just hoarding them as investments, driving up prices for people who actually want to use them.

Character 1: Seems like you're pretty invested in being unimpressed.

Character 2: [pausa] Look, I've watched too many people fall for equipment worship. They think buying the right gear will make them talented, like a five-thousand dollar guitar will turn them into Hendrix. It's all displacement activity to avoid actually practicing.

Character 1: [arrepentido] Maybe some people just enjoy nice things.

Character 2: Enjoying and needing are different beasts entirely. Most "professional" equipment is designed to extract maximum cash from amateurs who want to feel legitimate. The pros use whatever works and don't obsess over specs.

Character 1: You're exhausting, you know that?

Character 2: [tos] Truth tends to be exhausting. Easier to believe the fairy tales about how this microphone or that lens will transform your art. Meanwhile, the best work often comes from limitations, not endless options.

Character 1: What about medical equipment? Surely quality matters there.

Character 2: Even there, half of it is overengineered nonsense sold at criminal markups. A blood pressure cuff that costs a hospital eight hundred dollars works exactly the same as the forty dollar one from the pharmacy. It's all about insurance billing codes.

Character 1: You must be fun at parties.

Character 2: [condescendiente] Parties are just another form of equipment worship, actually. People spending hundreds on decorations and catering to create an illusion of success. Real connection happens over instant coffee in someone's kitchen at 2 AM, not at some orchestrated event.
