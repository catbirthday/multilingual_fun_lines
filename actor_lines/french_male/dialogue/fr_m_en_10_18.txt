=== Translated Dialogue ===

Character 1 [en]: You're the one who found that injured owl last week?

Character 2 [en]: [cry] Yes, that was me. Poor thing had a fractured wing and severe dehydration. We managed to stabilize her but the recovery's going to take months.

Character 1 [en]: Is she going to make it?

Character 2 [en]: The prognosis is actually quite good now. She's a barred owl, incredibly resilient species. Started eating on her own yesterday which is a major milestone in raptor rehabilitation.

Character 1 [en]: How do you even get into this kind of work?

Character 2 [en]: [bored] Started with a biology degree, then specialized in wildlife conservation. Most people think it's all cuddling baby animals but it's mostly data collection, habitat assessments, and grant writing. The actual hands-on rescue work is maybe ten percent of what I do.

Character 1 [en]: [long pause] That sounds harder than I expected.

Character 2 [en]: [laugh loudly] Everyone says that! Last week I spent fourteen hours tracking coyote scat patterns. Not exactly glamorous but those migration patterns are crucial for understanding urban wildlife adaptation.

Character 1 [en]: Coyote scat patterns? You're serious?

Character 2 [en]: Completely serious. Their droppings tell us everything about diet, territory, health status. We've discovered they're eating way more domestic fruit than expected, which means they're adapting to suburban food sources faster than predicted.

Character 1 [en]: I saw one in my backyard last month. Should I be worried?

Character 2 [en]: Not really, they're generally more afraid of you than you are of them. Just secure your trash cans and don't leave pet food outside. They're actually beneficial for controlling rodent populations.

Character 1 [en]: [very sad] My cat went missing around that time.

Character 2 [en]: That's concerning. Coyotes can prey on small pets, especially at dawn or dusk. Indoor cats that get outside are particularly vulnerable because they lack the defensive instincts of feral cats.

Character 1 [en]: I keep hoping she'll come back.

Character 2 [en]: Check with local shelters and veterinary clinics if you haven't already. Sometimes cats hide for weeks when scared. I've seen them return after everyone's given up hope.

Character 1 [en]: [overly polite] Thank you, that's very kind of you to say.

Character 2 [en]: The owl we rescued was actually found by a homeowner like you. She heard distress calls for three nights before investigating. Sometimes regular people make the biggest difference in wildlife rescue.

Character 1 [en]: Maybe I could volunteer somewhere?

Character 2 [en]: We always need help at the rehabilitation center. Fair warning though, it's mostly cleaning enclosures and preparing specialized diets. But you do get to see recoveries firsthand, which makes everything worthwhile.
