=== Original (English) ===

Character 1: My therapist keeps saying I should try anxiety meds but I don't really see the point.

Character 2: Wait, you're seeing a therapist for anxiety? I had no idea you were dealing with that stuff. How long has this been going on, and why didn't you mention it before?

Character 1: [chuckle] It's not a big deal. Everyone has anxiety these days, right?

Character 2: I mean yeah, but there's a difference between regular stress and actual anxiety disorders. Like my sister has GAD and it completely messes with her ability to function sometimes. The physical symptoms alone are brutal.

Character 1: [cry] Whatever. I just don't think pills are the answer to everything.

Character 2: Nobody's saying they're the answer to everything, but they can be incredibly helpful for some people. My roommate started SSRIs last year and it literally changed his life—he can actually leave the house without panicking now.

Character 1: [anxious] But don't those mess with your brain chemistry?

Character 2: Sure, but so does untreated anxiety. Your brain is already flooding itself with cortisol and adrenaline constantly. The meds just help regulate neurotransmitters that are already out of balance.

Character 1: I guess. The whole thing just seems overhyped.

Character 2: Overhyped? Anxiety disorders affect like 40 million adults in the US alone. That's almost 20% of the population dealing with something that significantly impacts their quality of life.

Character 1: People managed fine without medication for thousands of years.

Character 2: People also died from infected cuts and thought demons caused seizures. Modern medicine exists because we figured out better ways to treat things that were just accepted as inevitable suffering before.

Character 1: [very slowly] Maybe I'm just being stubborn about it.

Character 2: Look, medication isn't for everyone, and that's totally valid. But dismissing it entirely might be limiting your options. Have you tried other approaches like CBT or exposure therapy?

Character 1: My therapist mentioned CBT but it sounds like a lot of work.

Character 2: Yeah, it definitely requires effort, but it's basically retraining your brain's response patterns. My cousin did it for social anxiety and now she can actually give presentations at work without feeling like she's dying.

Character 1: Presentations don't bother me. It's more the random panic for no reason.

Character 2: [short pause] That's exactly what panic disorder is though. The whole point is that there's no logical trigger—your amygdala just decides it's time to freak out. That's why it's so exhausting to deal with.

Character 1: Fine, maybe I'll ask about the medication options next session.

Character 2: Just keep an open mind about it. Whether it's meds, therapy, or both, finding what works for you is what matters. Nobody should have to white-knuckle through constant anxiety when there are actual treatments available.
