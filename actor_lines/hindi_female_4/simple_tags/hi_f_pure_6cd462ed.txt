Character 2 [pure]: [crying while speaking] You knew her? You actually [pause] remember her?

Character 2 [pure]: She never told me about that promise. [regretful] Then again, there was a lot we didn't talk about near the end, both of us trying so hard to be strong for the other one.

Character 2 [pure]: [relieved] I thought if anyone found out it was me, they'd think I was trying to buy forgiveness or absolution. [exhales] But it was never about me, it was about making sure no parent had to choose between treatment and bankruptcy.

Character 1 [pure]: [firmly] You've saved lives, not just financially but literally, families who could focus on healing instead of bills. [very sincerely] Emma would be so proud of what you've built here in her memory.

Character 2 [pure]: [crying while speaking] Could I maybe come back sometimes? Not as the anonymous donor but just to volunteer, read stories to the kids like you said she wanted?

Character 1 [pure]: [delighted] We run volunteer orientation every Tuesday at ten, and I happen to know the coordinator personally. [warmly] I'll make sure you're assigned to the third floor, where Emma's Corner is, where her spirit already lives in everything you've done.

Character 2 [pure]: [relieved] Tuesday at ten. [long pause] Thank you for remembering her, for keeping that promise she made you keep, and for not letting me disappear into anonymity when maybe what I needed was to be part of this place again.
