=== Original (English) ===

Character 1: How do you always seem to handle everything that gets thrown at you?

Character 2: [speeding up] Adaptation isn't magic—it's pattern recognition accelerated. Every challenge has predecessors, echoes of problems already solved; I just connect the dots faster than most people bother to try.

Character 1: But some things are genuinely unprecedented, right?

Character 2: Nothing is truly unprecedented if you zoom out far enough. Evolution solved flight four separate times; nature rewrites the same solutions over and over with different ink.

Character 1: That's a pretty abstract way to think about daily problems.

Character 2: [confused] Wait, you think abstraction makes things harder? No no no—abstraction is the skeleton key; it unlocks every door because it strips away the irrelevant details.

Character 1: Give me a concrete example then.

Character 2: Lost your job? Same as a river changing course. The water doesn't panic; it finds the next lowest point and keeps flowing—resistance is what creates turbulence.

Character 1: [loudly] That's easy to say when you're not drowning!

Character 2: Drowning is fighting the water. Floating is accepting its rules and working within them—completely different energy expenditure, same liquid environment, radically different outcomes.

Character 1: You make it sound so simple.

Character 2: [angry] Simple doesn't mean easy! People conflate those constantly and it drives me insane—gravity is simple; escaping it took us thousands of years.

Character 1: Fair point. So how do you actually practice this adaptation thing?

Character 2: Start small. Change your route to work every day for a month; eat with your non-dominant hand; solve problems backwards—literally write the solution first then figure out the steps.

Character 1: Sounds exhausting honestly.

Character 2: [upset] Exhausting? Living rigid is exhausting! Fighting change burns more calories than surfing it—you're already spending the energy, just... inefficiently.

Character 1: Maybe I'm just not wired like you.

Character 2: Nobody's "wired" any particular way. Neuroplasticity means we're all constantly rewiring; most people just keep tracing the same circuits until they're trenches, then wonder why they feel stuck.

Character 1: [trailing off] I don't know if I believe that fully...

Character 2: Belief isn't required. Test it: one tiny adaptation tomorrow, then another—watch how quickly the impossible becomes mundane when you stop treating change like an enemy.
