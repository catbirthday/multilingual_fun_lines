=== Original (English) ===

Character 1: [passionately] I don't outline. Ever. Stories should breathe on their own.

Character 2: Oh wow, okay, so you're a total pantser then? I mean, that's brave—I tried writing without an outline once and, uhh, ended up with three different plots fighting for dominance by chapter ten.

Character 1: Plots are overrated. I follow character impulses.

Character 2: Hmm, but how do you handle, like, continuity issues? You know, when your protagonist suddenly has brown eyes instead of blue, or when you realize you've accidentally written them in two places at once?

Character 1: I don't revise until the end. Complete discovery.

Character 2: [mocking] Right, because publishers absolutely love receiving first drafts with zero structural editing! That's definitely how Stephen King got famous—just raw, unfiltered stream of consciousness straight to print.

Character 1: King actually doesn't outline. Look it up.

Character 2: Yeah no, you're right about that, but he also, err, writes like four hours every single day and has been doing it for decades—his instincts are basically hardwired at this point.

Character 1: Time doesn't create talent. Constraints kill creativity.

Character 2: Jeez, I mean, I guess that sounds poetic, but my agent literally won't even look at manuscripts without a clear three-act structure anymore—the market's just, uhh, too saturated for experimental stuff.

Character 1: Your agent sounds limiting. Why keep them?

Character 2: Because they've sold twelve of my books? Look, I get the whole artistic freedom thing, honestly, but mortgage payments don't really care about, you know, creative integrity.

Character 1: Twelve books. Are any of them actually yours?

Character 2: Ouch. Um, yeah, they're mine—I just happen to believe that structure and, like, genuine creativity aren't mutually exclusive concepts, you know?

Character 1: [extremely happy] Finally! Someone who'll debate properly! Most writers just nod along.

Character 2: Hah, well, I'm definitely not a nodder—spent too many workshop hours arguing about whether prologues are, mmm, inherently evil or just mostly evil to stay quiet now.

Character 1: Prologues are perfect. Sets mood without commitment.

Character 2: Oh man, see, that's where you lose me—readers skip them constantly! There's actual data showing that, uhh, something like sixty percent of people just flip right past to chapter one.

Character 1: Data ruins art. Who tracks reading habits anyway?

Character 2: Kindle does, actually—they track everything from reading speed to, err, where people abandon books most frequently, which is usually around page sixty-seven, weirdly enough.
