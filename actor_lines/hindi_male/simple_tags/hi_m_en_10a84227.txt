Character 1 [en]: [quickly] Yeah, and now I'm supposed to just figure it out myself somehow. [bitter] Dad doesn't understand any of this stuff and the state says I need to complete the semester or repeat the whole year.

Character 2 [en]: [warmly] Listen, I manage knowledge systems for a living, but more importantly, I helped your mom build that entire curriculum database she was using. [sighs] I can get you access to everything she organized.

Character 1 [en]: [frustrated] It's not just about access though—she knew exactly how to explain things when I got stuck, and she had this whole system for tracking my progress that I never paid attention to.

Character 2 [en]: [gently] She actually documented her teaching methods in detail, recorded explanations for difficult concepts, even left notes about your learning style. [pause] She was preparing for this possibility, though I don't think she told you that.

Character 1 [en]: [very quietly] She recorded herself teaching? [voice breaking] I had no idea she was that sick, she never let it show during our lessons.

Character 2 [en]: [tired] She'd come here after your sessions sometimes, completely drained, but still insisting on creating backup materials. [exhales] Said education was the one thing she could guarantee you'd have no matter what happened.

Character 1 [en]: [angry] Why didn't anyone tell me how bad it was? [loudly] I could have helped more, could have told her to rest instead of making all these recordings!

Character 2 [en]: [firmly] Because she wanted your last months together to be about learning, not about her illness. [softer] Those recordings aren't just lessons—they're her way of still being your teacher even now.

Character 1 [en]: [shaky voice] The house feels so empty without her voice explaining things, without her laughing at my terrible handwriting or making those analogies that actually made sense.

Character 2 [en]: [warmly] Well, her voice isn't gone—it's preserved in about two hundred hours of recordings, all categorized and tagged. [chuckles] She even recorded herself laughing at your handwriting in the penmanship improvement series.

Character 1 [en]: [surprised] Two hundred hours? [laughs] That's so like her, turning everything into a comprehensive system even when she was dying.

Character 2 [en]: [exhausted] Your mom was brilliant that way—she knew information architecture better than half my team. [pause] I can set you up with the full system today if you have time.

Character 1 [en]: [relieved] Yes, please.

Character 2 [en]: [quickly] Good, okay.

Character 1 [en]: [curious] Did she say anything about why she chose this approach? [quietly] Recording everything instead of just writing notes or using regular textbooks like other homeschool families?

Character 2 [en]: [thoughtfully] She said hearing someone's voice when you're confused is different from reading words on a page. [warmly] That connection matters, especially when the teacher really understands how your mind works. [pause]

Character 1 [en]: [very quietly] I'm scared I'll forget what she sounded like when she wasn't teaching, just talking normally about regular things. [sighs] These recordings are all I have left of her voice now.

Character 2 [en]: [gently] Then you should know she included personal messages too—not lessons, just her talking to you about life, mixed in between the academic content. [tired] She wanted you to have both the teacher and the mom.
