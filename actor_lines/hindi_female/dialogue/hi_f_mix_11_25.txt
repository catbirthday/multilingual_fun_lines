Character 1: So what's your take on these NIL deals in college sports?

Character 2: It's amateur athletics' funeral dressed up as a birthday party. We've basically admitted what everyone already knew—that college sports is a business—but somehow made it worse by pretending it's about "empowering athletes."

Character 1: You don't think athletes deserve to be compensated?

Character 2: Of course they deserve it. But let's not pretend this is about fairness. It's just shifting who gets to exploit eighteen-year-olds from universities to car dealerships and crypto bros.

Character 1: That's pretty harsh.

Character 2: Harsh? A quarterback at Alabama makes more than most CEOs now while his offensive line gets pizza vouchers. We've created a system where recruiting is literally just bidding wars between boosters with too much money.

Character 1: But isn't that better than the old system where they got nothing?

Character 2: Nothing? They got hundred-thousand-dollar educations, world-class training, national exposure. Now they get all that plus a Lamborghini for posting on Instagram twice a month about some energy drink nobody buys.

Character 1: You sound bitter about it.

Character 2: Not bitter, just realistic. Watch what happens to team chemistry when one kid drives a Bentley to practice while his teammates take the bus. This isn't solving problems, it's creating new ones with bigger price tags.

Character 1: [delighted] Oh come on, you must see some positives here!

Character 2: Sure, the transfer portal combined with NIL has created the most entertaining chaos in sports history. Nothing says "student-athlete" like switching schools three times chasing the highest bidder while majoring in general studies.

Character 1: [quickly] What about the kids who actually need the money though?

Character 2: Those kids aren't getting the deals. The money flows to five-star recruits who were already set, not the walk-on whose family actually needs help. It's trickle-down economics for teenagers.

Character 1: Do you think there's any way to fix it?

Character 2: Fix it? The toothpaste is out of the tube. We're heading straight toward semi-pro leagues with university logos, and honestly, maybe that's more honest than what we had before.
