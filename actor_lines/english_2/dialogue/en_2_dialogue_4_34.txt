Character 1: I don't— I really don't get why people are so obsessed with, with molecular gastronomy. It's just pretentious chemistry.

Character 1: But it's it's not even real cooking anymore, is it?

Character 2: Define "real cooking" though. Every time you whip egg whites into a meringue, you're doing molecular gastronomy—you're literally manipulating protein structures to trap air bubbles.

Character 2: [animated] No but seriously, think about it this way—when you reduce a stock for eight hours, you're concentrating flavors through evaporation, right? Foam is just another concentration method that happens to look dramatic on the plate!

Character 2: [trailing off] See that's the thing, most people judge molecular gastronomy based on bad Instagram photos and poorly executed attempts at fancy plating when really it's...

Character 2: Exactly my point! These "pretentious" techniques eventually become tools that help everyday cooks achieve better results, even if we're not making caviar pearls for Tuesday night dinner.
