=== Original (English) ===

Character 2: Oh, um, I work primarily in oil paints, but lately I've been, errr, experimenting with these mixed media pieces that incorporate found objects? Like, yesterday I literally pulled an old radiator grate from a dumpster and I'm thinking about how to integrate it into this piece about urban decay and, I dunno, resilience.

Character 1: A radiator grate? How does that even work with paint?

Character 2: Right, so, it's actually kind of fascinating because the texture creates these shadows that you can't really achieve with just canvas, you know? I mean, I build up layers of paint around the metal, letting it peek through in places, and it becomes this dialogue between the industrial and the organic.

Character 1: [very sincerely] Do you ever worry that people won't understand what you're trying to say?

Character 2: [shouting] God, yes! All the time! But here's the thing—art isn't supposed to be, like, immediately digestible, right? Sometimes the best pieces are the ones that make you uncomfortable at first, that force you to sit with them and, uh, really wrestle with what they're showing you.

Character 1: [belly laugh] Wrestling with art! That's a new one for me.

Character 2: Mmm, yeah, I know it sounds pretentious but honestly that's what it feels like sometimes, especially when I'm working on something really personal. Like, there's this one painting I've been avoiding finishing for three months because it's about my grandmother's dementia and every time I pick up the brush I just... I can't.

Character 1: [belly laugh] Three months? Your grandmother would probably tell you to get on with it.

Character 2: Oh jeez, you're absolutely right, she would! She was this incredibly practical woman who thought my art degree was, um, basically throwing money away, but she still came to every single show I had until she couldn't anymore, which was... that was something.

Character 2: [deep pitch] Ahhh, well, it wasn't really a choice, if that makes sense? Character 2: [regretful] Oof, there was this portrait I did of my ex-boyfriend where I was trying so hard to capture how I felt about him that I completely lost sight of, like, actual technique? It looked like a melted wax figure, and the worst part is I gave it to him as an anniversary gift before I realized how truly awful it was.
