=== Original (English) ===

Character 1: [stunned] Jesus Christ, how are you even breathing down here? The cargo hold's been sealed for three days and the air quality meter shows it's practically toxic.

Character 2: [wearily] I have not needed proper breath for four hundred and eighty-three years, Captain, though I appreciate your concern for my wellbeing.

Character 1: [backing away] Look, I don't know what kind of historical reenactment thing you're doing, but you need to get topside immediately before port authority finds you.

Character 2: [bitter laugh] Port authority concerns me far less than the weight of gold that sits beneath your ferry's hull, cursed metal that binds me to wherever it travels.

Character 1: [firmly] There's no gold on this vessel, we transport vehicles and passengers between Seattle and Bainbridge Island, nothing more, nothing less. [exhales] Now please, come with me.

Character 2: [measured] Check your manifest for a vintage Mercedes, license plate beginning with seven-seven, shipped from an estate sale in Santa Fe yesterday evening.

Character 1: [slowly] How could you possibly know about that vehicle when you've been trapped down here since before we left dock?

Character 2: [hollow] Because I feel every coin of Cortés's gold like fire in my bones, and that car's trunk holds twelve pieces from our raid on Tenochtitlan.

Character 1: That's impossible.

Character 2: [darkly amused] Many things are impossible, Captain, yet here I stand in your steel ship, wearing armor that should have rusted to dust centuries ago.

Character 1: [shaky] The Mercedes owner died last week of unknown causes, they said his body looked mummified despite being found within hours of death.

Character 2: [grim satisfaction] He thought he could sell the gold, break it into smaller pieces, spread the curse thin enough to escape it. [coughs] Fool.

Character 1: You're telling me there's actually cursed Aztec gold in a car on my ferry right now?

Character 2: [urgent whisper] Not Aztec, Captain—something far older that the Aztecs merely guarded, something that should have stayed buried beneath their temples forever.

Character 1: [desperate] We dock in twelve minutes and I have three hundred passengers aboard who have no idea they're traveling with—with whatever you are.

Character 2: [resigned] Then you must decide quickly whether to throw the Mercedes overboard and condemn me to the ocean floor, or let it reach shore and pass this burden to another. [pause] Choose wisely.

Character 1: Why should I believe any of this insanity when you could just be some disturbed person who snuck aboard?

Character 2: [calmly] Because in eleven minutes when we dock, you'll watch me walk through your solid steel hull like morning mist, still bound to follow that gold wherever it goes.

Character 1: [voice breaking] There has to be another way to break this curse, something that doesn't involve innocent people or destroying evidence of a crime. [laughs bitterly]

Character 2: [ancient exhaustion] If I knew another way, Captain, would I have spent five centuries watching everyone I meet age and die while I remain? [softer] The gold chooses its own path.
