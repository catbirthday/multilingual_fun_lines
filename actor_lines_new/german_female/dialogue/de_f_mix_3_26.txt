=== Original (English) ===

Character 1: So we finally got the user research results back from Q3. Have you had a chance to review them?

Character 2: I went through the whole deck yesterday, actually. The insights on our receiving workflows are pretty eye-opening—users are spending way more time on manual entry than we anticipated, especially during peak hours.

Character 1: The manual entry issue keeps coming up. What's your take on their proposed solutions?

Character 2: Their automation suggestions make sense on paper, but they're missing the reality of our warehouse constraints. We can't just implement barcode scanning everywhere when half our vendors still ship with handwritten labels—that's a fundamental disconnect in their recommendations.

Character 1: Did they interview any of your team directly?

Character 2: They talked to three of my receivers, but only during slow periods. That's like studying traffic patterns at 3 AM and thinking you understand rush hour—you need to see these guys when they're processing two hundred packages an hour.

Character 1: That seems like a pretty significant oversight. Should we push back on the findings?

Character 2: Not push back entirely, but definitely supplement them with our own data. I've got six months of time-motion studies that completely contradict their efficiency ratings, and those numbers came from actual peak season performance.

Character 1: How do you think upper management will react to conflicting data?

Character 2: [frustrated] Oh, they'll probably just cherry-pick whatever supports the narrative they already want. Look, I've been through four of these research cycles, and every time they ignore the operational realities we're dealing with down here.

Character 1: What specifically do you think they'll ignore this time?

Character 2: The staffing implications, for one. Their recommended system requires specialized training that would take my team offline for two weeks minimum—they just glossed over that like it's a minor detail.

Character 1: Two weeks of training seems excessive for a scanning system.

Character 2: [condescending] Well, clearly you haven't worked with enterprise inventory management systems before. This isn't some simple point-and-shoot scanner app—we're talking about integration with three different databases, custom exception handling, and compliance protocols that change by product category.

Character 1: [over confidently] I actually implemented a similar system at my last company. Took us four days.

Character 2: Four days for full implementation and training? Either you had a much simpler operation or you cut some serious corners, because proper change management alone should take longer than that.

Character 1: Maybe your team just needs better training materials?

Character 2: My team processes 98.7% accuracy rate with our current system. The problem isn't training materials—it's researchers who spend three hours in our facility and think they understand workflows we've been optimizing for years.

Character 1: So what do you suggest we do with this research?

Character 2: Take the user pain points seriously—those are valid—but run the solutions through people who actually understand operations. Otherwise we'll implement something that looks great in PowerPoint but falls apart the minute real volume hits.
