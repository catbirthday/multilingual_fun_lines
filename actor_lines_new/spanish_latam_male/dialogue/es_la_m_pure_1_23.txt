Character 1: Hi, I'm Alex. I heard you needed someone to help with the mural project?

Character 2: Yes! Perfect timing. I'm Jordan, the community center coordinator. We've been desperately looking for an actual artist who knows what they're doing with large-scale work.

Character 1: [very excited] I just finished a three-story piece downtown! The one with the birds?

Character 2: Wait, that's yours? The one on Fifth Street with the geometric birds that seem to move when you walk past? That piece is absolutely stunning. How did you even get the perspective to work like that?

Character 1: It's all about the vanishing points and color gradients. Took me six weeks though.

Character 2: [cough] Six weeks? That's actually pretty fast for something that size. Most muralists I've worked with take months for anything over two stories. Do you have a whole crew or do you work solo?

Character 1: [jealous] Solo mostly. Though I see you've worked with lots of artists before.

Character 2: A few over the years, but none with your reputation. Seriously, everyone's been talking about that Fifth Street piece. The way the morning light hits those metallics you used is just incredible.

Character 1: You've seen it at different times of day?

Character 2: Absolutely.

Character 1: [joking] Stalker much? Should I be worried?

Character 2: Ha! No, I just pass it on my commute. But I do sometimes take the long way home just to see how it looks at sunset. The orange really makes those blues pop in a way that's completely different from the morning.

Character 1: [friendly] That's exactly what I was going for! Most people don't notice the time-of-day effects.

Character 2: [exhausted] Well, after managing seventeen failed art installations this year where artists promised "interactive experiences" and delivered blank walls, I've learned to actually pay attention to the ones that work.

Character 1: Seventeen failures? That sounds rough.

Character 2: [angry] You have no idea! The last guy literally just spray-painted a smiley face and called it "minimalist expression." Twenty thousand dollar budget! And then he had the nerve to invoice us for "conceptual development time."

Character 1: [sarcastic] Wow, a smiley face. How revolutionary. Real cutting-edge stuff there.

Character 2: Right? So when I heard you were available, I practically sprinted to make this meeting happen. Your portfolio is exactly what we need - actual skill, actual vision, actual completed projects that don't look like my nephew's finger paintings.

Character 1: So what's the wall like? Size, texture, sun exposure?

Character 2: It's forty feet wide, twenty-two feet tall, south-facing brick. Gets full sun from noon to sunset. The surface is pretty rough but we can prep it however you need. Budget's flexible if you can deliver something even half as good as Fifth Street.
