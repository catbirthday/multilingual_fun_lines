Character 1 [en]: Seventeen protocols? That's oddly specific. You seem to know a lot about this stuff.

Character 2 [en]: [gasp] Because I read publicly available information! The Snowden leaks, congressional reports, privacy advocacy websites—it's all out there. You don't need security clearance to understand how surveillance infrastructure works.

Character 1 [en]: [high-pitched] Maybe YOU'RE the NSA agent! This whole conversation is just you trying to throw me off your trail!

Character 2 [en]: [shouting] Oh for the love of—if I were NSA I'd have much better things to do than debate surveillance ethics with someone who thinks the government cares about their shower singing! Real intelligence work is mind-numbingly boring paperwork and data analysis!

Character 1 [en]: [flirtatious] Wow, you're really passionate about this. It's kind of attractive when you get all worked up about privacy rights.

Character 1 [en]: Panopticon? Now you're just showing off with fancy words.

Character 2 [en]: It's Bentham's concept of a prison where inmates can't tell if they're being watched, so they behave as if they always are. Foucault expanded it to describe how surveillance shapes modern society. Basic philosophy really.

Character 1 [en]: [very quietly] Okay but seriously, should I put tape over my laptop camera or is that just paranoid?
