Character 1: [coldly] Sensationalist? We have soil samples showing benzene levels forty times above EPA limits, and your client's own internal memos discussing quote unquote disposal solutions that would save millions.

Character 2: [quickly] Those documents were obtained illegally and you know it, which means they're inadmissible and publishing them would open you up to serious legal consequences. [pause] I'm trying to protect you here, believe it or not.

Character 1: [sarcastically] Oh, how noble of you, protecting me while children in that neighborhood are developing rare cancers at five times the national average. [angry] Where's your protection for them, counselor?

Character 2: [quietly] Stop it, Sarah.

Character 1: Remember when we stayed up all night writing that petition against the fracking company senior year? [bitter] You said environmental justice was going to be your life's work, that you'd never sell out to the corporate machine.

Character 2: [regretful] That was before I understood how the real world works, before I had responsibilities beyond idealistic campus protests. [exhales] My mother's cancer treatments aren't going to pay for themselves, and public interest law doesn't cover experimental therapies.

Character 1: [softly] You never told me about your mother. [pause] God, Jess, why didn't you call me when she got sick instead of taking blood money from these environmental criminals?

Character 2: [shaky voice] Because calling you wouldn't have paid for her oncologist or kept our house from foreclosure when she couldn't work anymore. [bitter] Your righteous podcast income doesn't exactly translate to six-figure medical bills, does it?

Character 1: [empathetically] There had to be other ways, other firms that aren't actively destroying communities. [sighs] You're brilliant, Jess, any environmental law group would have hired you in a heartbeat.

Character 2: [frustrated] Environmental law groups offering forty thousand a year when I needed three times that just to keep Mom alive? [laughs bitterly] You still don't get it, do you, living in your black and white world of heroes and villains.

Character 1: [analytically] What I get is that Hanson Industries has a pattern of targeting low-income neighborhoods for illegal dumping because they know those families can't afford lengthy legal battles. That's not gray area, that's predatory.

Character 2: [firmly] And what you're missing is that without proper legal process, your vigilante journalism could destroy any chance of actual justice. [pause] A mistrial because of your podcast's inflammatory coverage helps no one, especially not those families.

Character 1: [confused] Wait, mistrial?

Character 2: [very quietly] The EPA's building a criminal case, has been for months now, but they need everything done perfectly by the book or Hanson walks free forever. [hesitantly] Your podcast could compromise two years of federal investigation if you publish those leaked documents.

Character 1: [stunned] You're feeding information to the EPA while publicly defending Hanson? [gasps softly] Jesus, Jess, if they find out what you're doing, your career's over, maybe worse than that.

Character 2: [warmly] Some things are worth the risk, Sarah, you taught me that once. [pause] But I need you to sit on this story for three more weeks until the indictments are sealed, can you do that for those families?
