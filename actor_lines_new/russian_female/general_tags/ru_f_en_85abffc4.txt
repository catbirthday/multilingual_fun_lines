Scenario: A lab technician discovers that the anonymous donor funding their rare disease research for the past five years is the old money heiress whose daughter the technician failed to save a decade ago.

Character 2 [en]: [quietly] I've been following your work since you published that paper on pediatric mitochondrial disorders, the one where you mentioned your greatest failure was losing a patient to an undiagnosed variant.

Character 1 [en]: That patient was Emily. Your daughter Emily. I still have nightmares about that day when we couldn't figure out what was wrong in time.

Character 2 [en]: [steadily] I know who she was to me, Dr. Chen, and I know you did everything possible with the knowledge and resources available at the time.

Character 1 [en]: [voice breaking] But we didn't save her, and here you are, funding the very research that might have—God, how can you even stand to look at me?

Character 2 [en]: Seventeen children are alive today because of your research, Dr. Chen. [exhales slowly] Seventeen families who won't know what we know.

Character 1 [en]: [softly] You've read all my reports then, followed every case, and you never once interfered or made yourself known until today.

Character 2 [en]: [warming] I wanted the work to be yours, untainted by obligation or guilt, just pure science driven by the desire to solve what we couldn't solve before.

Character 1 [en]: [overwhelmed] Mrs. Whitmore, I need you to know that Emily changed everything about how I approach medicine, how I think about rare diseases, about listening to parents.

Character 2 [en]: [gently] She would have loved knowing that, loved being part of something bigger, even in this way. She always wanted to help people, just like her favorite doctor.

Character 1 [en]: [choking up] Me?

Character 1 [en]: And you believed I would, enough to invest millions in a grieving, guilt-ridden lab tech who couldn't save your only child.

Character 2 [en]: I invested in the person who held her hand for fourteen hours straight, who fought insurance companies, who called specialists at three in the morning begging for ideas.

Character 1 [en]: [releasing breath] All this time, I thought I was running from my failure, but really, I've been running toward something with your help, haven't I?

Character 2 [en]: [peacefully] We both have, Dr. Chen. Every child you save carries a piece of Emily forward, and that's the only memorial I ever wanted.

Character 1 [en]: [gratefully firm] Then let's save more of them, together this time, with your name on the research if you'll allow it.

Character 2 [en]: Emily Chen-Whitmore Foundation has a nice ring to it, don't you think? [soft laugh] She would have insisted on the hyphen.
